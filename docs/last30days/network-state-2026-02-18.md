## Steelman vs criticism (useful framing)
### Steelman (pro Network State)
- **Exit as leverage**: If legacy nation-states become sclerotic or authoritarian, digitally-coordinated exit (pooling resources to build alternatives) is a peaceful, reproducible mechanism for competition and innovation.
- **Values-aligned communities**: Network states let people self-sort by ideology, culture, or mission (e.g., longevity research, crypto experimentation, free speech maximalism) rather than being stuck in geographical accidents of birth.
- **Tech enables coordination at scale**: Web3 tools (DAOs, smart contracts, NFT-based membership) make it feasible to organize, fundraise, and govern without needing a traditional bureaucracy.
- **Diplomatic recognition is possible**: Balaji argues that if a network state achieves critical mass (population, GDP, land), existing nations will pragmatically recognize it (like how new countries form after decolonization or secession).

### Criticism (skeptics / "SV utopianism")
- **Nation-states won't cede power**: Sovereignty isn't something you crowdfundâ€”it's violently contested. Governments have militaries, legal monopolies, and zero incentive to let tech billionaires carve out autonomous zones.
- **History of failure**: Smart cities, charter cities, seasteadingâ€”all have failed or underdelivered. Zanzibar will likely join the graveyard. The pattern: hype, capital flight, local resentment, regulatory capture or collapse.
- **Ideological homogeneity is fragile**: Filtering for "like-minded individuals" creates echo chambers and cult dynamics, not resilient societies. Diversity and disagreement are features, not bugs.
- **Tech-solutionism ignores politics**: You can't code your way out of governance. Real cities need sanitation, dispute resolution, emergency services, social safety netsâ€”unglamorous work that crypto enthusiasts ignore.
- **Oligarchic capture risk**: Network states = billionaire fiefdoms. Instead of democratic accountability, you get "founder as dictator" with a DAO veneer.
- **Opus Dei / reactionary agenda**: Critics see the movement as a techno-libertarian project aligned with religious conservatism (Opus Dei) and anti-democratic impulses.

## Glossary / positioning map
- **Network State**: A concept from Balaji Srinivasan describing a digitally-organized community that crowdfunds physical territory, builds its own institutions, and seeks diplomatic recognition as a new sovereign entity.
- **Balaji Srinivasan**: Former Coinbase CTO, a16z GP, tech entrepreneur. Wrote *The Network State* (2022). Central evangelist for the idea.
- **Network School**: Balaji's initiative to run cohorts and experiments in network state building (e.g., "Anthropological Exit" March 2026 cohort).
- **Smart cities**: Umbrella term for tech-infused urban projects (AI traffic, IoT sensors, etc.). Often conflated with network states, but distinctâ€”most smart cities are municipal upgrades, not sovereignty projects.
- **Charter cities**: Similar conceptâ€”build new cities with special legal/governance frameworks. Examples: Honduras's PrÃ³spera (struggling), Dubai (successful but not "new sovereignty"). Network states extend this with digital-first coordination.
- **Seasteading**: Precursor movement (Peter Thiel-funded) to build floating cities in international waters. Failed to launch viable projects; network states are "seasteading 2.0" but on land.
